Everyone has their favorite vegetables to roast. My list includes pumpkins of various kinds when in season -- including the basic pumpkin, butternut squash, acorn squash, and spagetti squash; carrots, parsnip, potatoes, onions. Peel, wash and dry them and chop into relatively large chunks. Season with olive oil, salt and pepper and if you are a fan of garlic, add in a dash of it too! Roast in the oven at 375F for about an hour, until vegetables are tender and falls apart from a fork easily. I like mine with a little brown crust on top, but one needs to be careful to have this -- a little less oil does the trick, but it is an art to balance the amount of oil. Use thyme or rosemary sprigs, or chopped coriander leaves for garnish.
